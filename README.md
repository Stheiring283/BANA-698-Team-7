# BANA-698-Team-7
This is the repository for team 7's project for BANA 698
# Drivers of High-Effort Engagement on Social Media

## Project Overview

This project examines the structural, behavioral, and algorithmic
factors associated with **high-effort engagement** on social media.

High-effort engagement refers to deeper user actions such as: - Link
clicks\
- Shares\
- Saves\
- Other intentional interactions beyond passive likes

Using a dataset of approximately 10,000 social media posts, the project
analyzes how early engagement patterns, influencer characteristics, and
algorithmic amplification features relate to high-effort engagement
outcomes.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

## Research Streams

### Sustained Engagement

**Research Question:**\
To what extent is sustained engagement associated with higher rates of
high-effort user interactions such as link clicks and shares on social
media?

Sustained engagement is defined as: - High engagement within the first
60 minutes of posting\
- Strong total engagement levels over time

**Researcher:** Shaun\
**Title:** Sustained Engagement on Social Media: The Relationship
Between Early Interaction and High-Effort User Actions

------------------------------------------------------------------------

### Influencer Characteristics & Activity Dynamics

**Research Question:**\
To what extent do influencer characteristics and activity dynamics
predict high-effort engagement on social media?

This stream focuses on creator-level attributes such as: - Authority\
- Posting frequency\
- Activity patterns

**Researcher:** Ameya\
**Title:** Influencer Characteristics and Activity Dynamics as
Predictors of High-Effort Engagement on Social Media

------------------------------------------------------------------------

### Algorithmic Amplification

**Research Question:**\
To what extent are algorithmic amplification features associated with
high-effort engagement?

This stream examines platform-driven exposure mechanisms such as: -
Reach\
- Impressions\
- Amplification metrics

**Researcher:** Isaac\
**Title:** The Role of Algorithmic Amplification in Driving High-Effort
Engagement

------------------------------------------------------------------------

## Methodology

Because high-effort engagement is a count-based outcome that is highly
skewed and overdispersed, Negative Binomial regression models are used
to estimate associations between predictors and engagement levels.
